Ives Street Washing Machine!

Welcome to this exploration of the Ives Street Washing Machine, a public washing machine interface used by many residents on Ives Street daily.

  • Who are this machine's users?
  • How do they use it?
  • What do they think of the design of the device?

This webpage features a sketch of the interface, interview questions asked of washing machine users, and their summarized responses. I then those insights to develop two captivating personas, complete with a storyboard for one of them.

Let's dive in!

Interface Sketch

These are sketches of the washing machine interface.

The caption, rewritten for clarity: Varying types of dirty/soiled clothing are homogenously grouped according to user-selected specifications, and placed in this washing machine along with detergent to be washed and cleaned. Key components are various dials to control relevant settings: soil level for extent of clean needed, wash temp for water temperature, load type, rinse options, and water level. These should all be used in conjunction to configure the machine for optimal wash settings as to properly wash while not damaging clothing.

Observations & Interviews

This section details close observations and conversational interviews conducted with users of the washing machine to understand their experience, expectations, motives, and challenges.

The following are my key observations regarding users' patterns in their interaction with the interface, sorted by differentiating observations and similar patterns in interactions:

  • Varying speeds with which users interacted with key settings: user 1 took almost no time, just setting the load to normal, user 2 took a lot more time, considering each slowly, while user 3 took less time than user 2, but considered settings more than user 1
  • Varying accuracy with when returned to the machine to see if load completed: user 1 was almost completely accurate, user 2 was 15 minutes early then 15 minutes late, and user 3 was 20 minutes late - causing negative facial reactions, more so than any other part of the process
  • Pods vs detergent: user 1 used a pod, user 2 used both, and user 3 used just liquid detergent
  • Neutral reactions: reactions when setting interface settings initially were mostly neutral
  • Consistently, the water level setting was never touched
  • Each person followed a standard order of operations: users opened the lid, put their clothes in with relative ease, put detergent (either a pod or liquid), closed the lid, set settings in less than a minute, then started the load, overall taking an average of a minute and a half

The following are questions that I asked:

  1. What is your name, how old are you, and how long have you been doing your laundry?
  2. Describe your laundry process: how do you decide you need to do laundry, when do you do it and how often, how long does it take you, and what are you most commonly washing?
  3. How does your experience using this washing machine interface compare to others you might’ve used in the past? If you’ve never used one before, how was the process of first using the machine?
  4. Is there anything most notable about the design of this machine: any features, control panel settings, or settings that come to mind?
  5. Have you faced any challenges using the interface that’ve caused you to want to change something about it: is there anything most notably absent from the design of this machine?
  6. Would you recommend the design of this laundry machine to other washing machine developers?
  7. Anything else you'd like to add?

This is a summary hilighting main points and important patterns from the responses:

  • Every person has a behavioral and emotional routine associated with laundry in terms of when they decide and how they do it: user 1 considers it very procedural and chore-like, hence their hastened and cyclical once-a-week, after work process; user 2 enjoys taking care of their clothes and devotes a morning at home to do two loads to start the day; user 3 allows sporadic desires to clean or practical considerations of running out of clean clothes to run large loads to finish as quick as possible.
  • The design of this machine is more complex, customizable, and effective than what users had experienced before, specifically comparing to standard dorm washing machines. Every person noted that this machine was better than the dorms’.
  • Slower speeds at which each person chose settings and their appreciation for a variety of settings both correlate to a deeper relationship with clothes themselves: user 2, who took the longest to choose settings, noted the various options for loads, and most emphatically noted hatred of the dorm machines, indicated the most care for their clothing, while user 1, who took less than 10 seconds for the entire order of operations, noted a cyclical wardrobe of repeating sweatshirts and t-shirts that they didn’t care much about.
  • Independent and interactive dials allow users with varying preferences and depth of experience ease of use and function: user 1 did not care to specify settings and was still able to start load easily, user 2 could specify and noted that it seemed easy to understand for new users, and user 3 noted that other machines have default settings for each type of load, but independent dials in this machine allow users ease of customization.
  • Most notable frustrations include lack of indicator of load duration, compartment for detergent, and a max fill indicator for load size, features repeatedly considered missing that could improve the design and overall effectiveness.

Personas

This section explores personas of two users Unexperienced Ursula and Experienced Ethan using empathy maps:

Storyboard

Last but not least, I present the story of Unexperienced Ursula's journey using the washing machine, struggling as she uses a non-dorm machine for the first time, while trying to build the best routine for washing her new clothes:

Ursula has been out of her dorm and moved into a new house (with an in-unit, free washer) for almost 3 weeks. She is running out of clean clothes and wants to wash the cute new sweaters her mom just bought her, but doesn’t want to mess them up. She also needs more cotton t-shirts, jeans, and underclothes which are all just mixed in her hamper.

She sorts her clothes and groups them in similar piles, feeling confident. She's used a washer before, how hard could this be?

In the basement with her sweater and t-shirt loads, Ursula is shocked to discover a washing machine with the most dials she's ever seen and nowhere to put her liquid detergent.

Ursula thinks back to the dorm washing machine. While it was easier to use, it only had 3 options and shrunk her favorite sweater.

Determined to not do the same to her new sweaters, she decides to run her t-shirts first, turning the dials to where they start and pouring liquid detergent.

She starts the load, but she wonders, has it really started? How long will it take? She decides to come back in 30 minutes to check.

Meanwhile, she googles the best settings to wash her new Banana Republic sweaters.

After 30 minutes, she descends the stairs to the basement, sees the load is not done, and feels slightly frustrated.

One hour later, she descends the stairs once more to find her t-shirts washed and cold. Success!

She configures the settings the internet gave her for her sweaters and prays for the best.

One hour later, her sweaters are successfully washed and not shrunk!

Ursula runs 2 more loads: underclothes and jeans, scared that she might be overfilling the machine.

While she waits, she does her homework assignment due at midnight, grateful for breaks where she checks her laundry!

At last, everything has been washed (and dried), and returned to her closet. Ursula smiles gleefully - success! There's a lot she doesn't know... but it'll only get better from here!
